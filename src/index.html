Election VR

Data visualizations of election data in virtual reality

by Steve Brudz of Earthling Interactive

Overview

The 2016 presidential election results show Donald Trump winning the electoral college but Hillary Clinton winning the popular vote. The existing visualizations showing states or counties as red or blue provides information about the majority vote but they hide any sense of scale and they conceal the number of votes that non-majority candidates received.

The goal of this project is to build a visualization that leverages the 3D capabilities of virtual reality to show a more complete and nuanced view of the voting results.

If you have a mobile phone and a Google Cardboard headset (or a VR system like the HTC Vive or Occulus), you can view the project in VR. You can also view it on a PC and use the mouse and w-a-s-d keys to navigate.

Election Data Visualizations

  • 2D Election Map - A 2D map of the US showing the popular vote in the 2016 US Presidential election
  • VR Election Towers - Tower visualization in VR showing the popular vote in the 2016 US Presidential election
  • VR Election Map - US Map visualization in VR showing the popular vote in the 2016 US Presidential election
